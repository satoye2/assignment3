Percent Change in Black-American
Population

1910 - 1970

This map shows the overall percent change in Black population in every major U.S. city. This data spans from 1910-1970 during a period known as known as The Great Migration.

During the Great Migration, over 6 million Black Americans migrated from the rural south to the urban, industrial north. Cities like Chicago, Detroit and Washington DC attracted many Black Americans.

For example, In Newark, NJ, the Black population increased by 52%, while in Tallahassee, FL, the Black population decreased by 39%.


Data Source: "The Great Migration, 1910 to 1970" United States Census Bureau
